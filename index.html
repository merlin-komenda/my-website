How I think
Four things I come back to every time.
01
Name the outcome first.
Before any work starts, we agree on what success looks like. Not what we'll do. What we'll achieve.
02
Reduce the burden.
The best systems remove friction from the people inside them. If it's hard to use, it won't be used.
03
Build for the room you're not in.
A system that needs you present to function isn't a system. It's a dependency.
04
Ship, learn, adjust.
Not because iteration is trendy. Because nothing survives first contact with reality unchanged.
Structure beats intensity.
Clarity comes before automation.
Good systems survive turnover.
