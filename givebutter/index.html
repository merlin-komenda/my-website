Who I am

I started at GiveGab — working directly with nonprofits every day in support, then CS, then implementation. I know what it feels like when onboarding breaks for an organization running on three staff members and a mission they've built their lives around. That's not abstract to me.

From there I made a deliberate move into a different sector. Zappi is data-heavy, enterprise-facing, a genuinely different kind of customer and a different kind of pressure. I wanted to see what my thinking looked like when I couldn't rely on sector familiarity. What I came back with was sharper — better process instincts, cleaner systems thinking, a clearer sense of where implementation functions actually break down and why.

At Zappi that meant inheriting a delivery motion built around what worked for us internally, not what got the customer to value. So we changed it. Stopped asking customers to carry setup decisions they didn't have context for yet. Started leading with recommendations instead of questions. Applied AI at the specific friction points where it actually helped — only after the process was clear enough to support it.
